Introduction


Foreword


This amendment, intending to be the 28th amendment to the United States Constitution, has been written for the purpose of improving mental health care in the United States of America (USA). This amendment shall have a dual purpose, of both providing better mental health education, and also contributing to universal health care across the united states, and all of its private insurances.


Necessary Information


Every year 11% of adults with a mental illness(es) are uninsured, and 21% of adults are experiencing a(n) mental illness. The youth have not fared as well either, with around 10-20% of youth experiencing depressive episodes in 2023 alone.


Proposed Amendments


Section I. Mental health Education

The people shall have a greater right to learn about mental health and what they can do to help its cause using advanced methods.


Section II. Mental health funding

The taxes for Mental Health shall be affordable,


Section III. Mental health coverage for private insurances

Mental health shall be funded by other taxes, and shall not have its own tax.


Closing Information


Date of effect

This amendment shall go into effect on the date the 3 branches vote on. This shall not be delayed unless otherwise spoken and agreed upon by the people, or 2 of the 3 branches.


Conclusion

The people shall have the right to learn, afford, and live their life without worry of mental health illnesses.
